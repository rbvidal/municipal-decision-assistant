The Federal Building Code (BauGB) provides the legal framework for urban planning in Germany. Section 1 mandates that municipalities prepare land-use plans to guide urban development. Section 30 defines the admissibility of building projects within development plan areas. Section 34 governs building within existing built-up areas (unplanned inner areas). Section 35 addresses building in outer areas, generally restricting non-agricultural construction. Development plans (Bebauungspläne) are legally binding and define permitted uses, building heights, density, and design standards. The plan must be adopted by the district council and published.
